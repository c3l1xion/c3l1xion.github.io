The biggest mistake I have made (thus far) in my journey in hacking is attempting to learn how to do so with a negative mindset. It may sound like a bunch of malarky, motivational speeches to make you feel better, but it's true: if your mind is in the wrong place and you keep demeaning yourself, you won't go anywhere on this journey.

You call yourself stupid; you get angry at yourself and loathe your mind for doing what? Trying to improve, trying to be better, trying to understand how a system works? Your brain is a living machine that takes all thoughts and treats them like instructions. If you call yourself stupid, you are stupid. If you say this will be difficult, you have already set yourself up for failure.

If you view hacking as a game, a puzzle game, to be specific, you will start to enjoy yourself and become motivated to break into the machine or find some sort of exploit that will allow you access.
If you view yourself as capable, powerful, and clever, your mind will shift your thoughts to fit your views, and in turn, your actions will shift to reflect what you were thinking.

** This is the power of the infinite feedback loop. **
Everything in life reflects and interacts with each other on levels that you cannot comprehend. Study the little parts in nature and notice how they apply to you. Your mind will always find ways to reaffirm your confirmation bias, if you change that mindset to fit a more positive, and productive lifestyle your mind will look for all the good parts during your journey and not gravitate towards the negative parts.
I implore you to humor my ideas during this first step and see how your life and effectiveness in this journey change.
